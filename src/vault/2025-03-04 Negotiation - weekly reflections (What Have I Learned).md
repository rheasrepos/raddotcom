Rhea Madhogarhia
My weekly reflections: What Have I Learned?
To start, I’d like to say that I rarely left a simulation/negotiation in this class unhappy. The only exception
was during a simulation I admittedly did not excitedly engage in (due to sleepiness). This brings me to my
first point - demeanor and attitude proved to be very important in these simulations. Whether we like it or
not, in a negotiation, your interlocutor is perceiving you, predicting your moves, and attempting to mold
you to their expectations. So, appearing collaborative, relaxed/assured, sincere, and honest were all
extremely helpful to me. While I left satisfied most of the time, I was led to doubt my tactics when shown
class data.. But, when we started talking about prioritizing interest over position, I began to rest assured
that as long as I negotiated at or above my reservation price (a price I made sure not to lowball), avoided
no agreement (especially because we had no control over our BATNA), and stayed civil, my satisfaction
with my results felt justified. I took an iterative approach to refining my strategy to satisfy interest. Week
1 we discussed anchoring and first offers, which compelled me to speak first but ask for an offer before I
could be asked for one. In the second week, I played to emotions and tried to inspire a bit of guilt but
overall felt lost and confused about how valuable the numbers we were throwing around actually were.
So, during week 3, I opened the discussion by asking us to each discuss our priorities and interests. While
this negotiation (Bullard House) was tricky (lying, on behalf of a client, no great deal), I felt that this was
one of my most amicable and creative negotiations. Starting off the rest of negotiations with a gauge of
interest laid good groundwork for honesty and collaboration. It felt like negotiation was less about what
number we could haggle to, but instead, how do we frame and fit all these pieces together to satisfy all of
our interests. Negotiations became more of a puzzle than a competition to maximise earnings. While I
learned many smaller lessons along the way, the last lesson I think that stuck with me was to be
reasonable. While this could be lumped under honesty, my role as Venus in the Coalition Game allowed
me to realize that I felt best about a negotiation when I knew how,, when, and to what extent I could use
my leverage. TLDR; Reconcile interests, not positions. Cooperation inspires!
Remember your BATNA. Be someone you’d want to negotiate with!
